ReVITS: Retrieval-Augmented Text-to-Speech for Phonation Style Transfe

Abstract: Neural Text-to-Speech (TTS) systems have made significant progress in generating neutral and natural-sounding speech. Yet, the synthesis of expressive speech, especially with fine control over the output style, remains challenging. We propose ReVITS, a novel approach that augments a TTS model with a retrieval step, enabling controlled and fine-grained phonation style transfer. This method leverages a database of reference utterances to define a target style. By incorporating an interpolation parameter, ReVITS facilitates a seamless blend of the original and target styles, thereby expanding the expressive capabilities of the system. We investigate the potential of the proposed approach through a study on whispered speech style transfer. Both objective and subjective evaluations demonstrate the proposed approach's efficacy in gradually adapting the TTS output to the target style. However, we also observe a trade-off between preserving speech naturalness and attaining style similarity.
